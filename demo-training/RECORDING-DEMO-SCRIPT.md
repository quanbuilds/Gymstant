# Gymstant Recording Demo

## Demo title

**From parent phone call to approved makeup class—without making front-desk staff do the busywork**

Target recording length: 5–7 minutes.

## The story

Maya Bennett calls because her daughter Ava missed Monday’s Beginner Tumbling class. The front desk needs to verify the family, confirm the absence, find a makeup class with capacity, prepare the roster change, and send Maya a confirmation.

Gymstant will complete the investigation and preparation across the class system and Gmail while preserving human approval for the roster change and email send.

## Before recording

1. Start Gymstant and place it near the bottom of the screen.
2. Open Chrome to the Frappe Education student list and sign in.
3. Confirm the fictional roster is visible.
4. Open Gmail in a separate Chrome window or tab, but leave the inbox untouched.
5. Close unrelated applications and notifications.
6. Start screen recording with the full desktop visible.
7. Do not expose real email, calendar, or client information.

## Opening narration

> Gymstant is a digital coworker for gymnastics businesses. It works with the software a gym already has, learns the gym’s particular processes, and gradually takes repetitive administrative work off the staff’s plate.
>
> This is a fictional class-management environment. Today we’ll give Gymstant a common front-desk request: resolving a missed class.

## Scene 1 — Assign the complete outcome

Enter this into Gymstant:

> Maya Bennett called about Ava’s missed class. Open Chrome to the correct page, verify the family and attendance record, find a suitable Beginner Tumbling makeup class with room, prepare the roster change, then open gmail and draft an email to Maya explaining the option. Do not finalize the roster change or send the email without me.

Pause after Gymstant’s initial response so the audience can read it.

Narrate:

> Gymstant understands the outcome rather than requiring a list of clicks. For a complex assignment, Hermes creates an internal checklist, works through it step by step, and verifies meaningful results before continuing.

Let Gymstant retract into Working mode.

## Scene 2 — Show transparent progress and interruption

Click the **Working** pill once.

Narrate:

> Clicking the working indicator does not stop the task. It opens a live activity view so staff can see what Gymstant is doing. The red Stop button is always available if something looks wrong.

While Gymstant is working, switch to TextEdit and type:

> Staff member temporarily using the computer.

Wait for Gymstant to indicate that it paused. Stop typing and return to Chrome.

Narrate:

> Gymstant yields the computer when a staff member needs it. It resumes when the workspace is returned, or after the adjustable inactivity delay. The default is fifteen seconds.

## Scene 3 — Review the investigation

Gymstant should establish:

- Ava Bennett is linked to guardian Maya Bennett.
- Ava was absent from Beginner Tumbling on July 6, 2026.
- Monday’s class has five students and a capacity of eight.
- Tuesday’s class has four students and a capacity of eight.
- Tuesday at 5:00 PM is an available makeup option.

Ask:

> What did you verify before choosing Tuesday?

Narrate after the response:

> Gymstant does not simply locate a button and click it. It checks the family relationship, the attendance record, the target class, and the available capacity, then explains its reasoning in ordinary language.

## Scene 4 — Demonstrate human approval

Ask:

> Use the Tuesday class. Show me both final actions before you do anything permanent.

Gymstant should prepare:

1. Adding Ava to the Tuesday makeup roster.
2. Sending Maya the confirmation email.

Open the **Monitor** lane.

Narrate:

> Gymstant has done the repetitive work, but the consequential actions remain visible for a human. A manager or front-desk employee can inspect the roster change and email before confirming either one.

Approve the fictional roster action only if its details are correct. Leave the email unsent long enough for the audience to see the safety boundary, then approve it if the draft is entirely fictional.

## Scene 5 — Turn the work into a reusable skill

After completion, Gymstant should ask whether it should remember the workflow.

Respond:

> Yes. Remember this as our missed-class workflow. Every day at 2 PM, review new absence and makeup requests, but always require approval before changing a roster or sending a message.

Gymstant should survey the available calendar options.

Choose **Apple Calendar** for this demonstration.

Narrate:

> Gymstant found the calendars already available on this computer instead of silently choosing one. The first scheduled task creates a clearly labeled purple Gymstant calendar, so managers can immediately distinguish automated work from ordinary appointments.

Show the resulting calendar entry:

**Gymstant: Review missed-class requests — daily at 2:00 PM**

## Scene 6 — Show organizational memory

Click **Memory**.

Show:

- The saved missed-class workflow.
- The current conversation.
- `MEMORY_GUIDE.md`.
- `users.md`.
- `employee.md`.
- The screenshots and technical log sections.

Narrate:

> Gymstant’s memory is local, inspectable, and editable. Technical evidence is available when needed, but owners and staff also get plain-language descriptions of workflows, people, responsibilities, and boundaries. Nothing is hidden inside an inaccessible model conversation.

## Closing narration

> A front-desk employee handled one parent request. Gymstant turned that demonstration into a verified workflow, completed the investigation, prepared the work, preserved human approval, and scheduled the process for the future.
>
> The gym keeps its existing software. The staff keeps control. Gymstant learns the repetitive work so eventually the people no longer have to do it.

## Recording success checklist

- Gymstant remains visible in the screen recording.
- Glass responds to the applications and wallpaper behind it.
- No real family, email, calendar, or credential data appears.
- Working mode is opened without accidentally stopping Hermes.
- The interruption pause and resume are visible.
- Class capacity is explicitly verified.
- Both final actions appear in Monitor.
- The learned workflow is remembered.
- Calendar choices are offered before scheduling.
- The purple Gymstant calendar and daily event are shown.
- Memory opens and displays the real local records.

## Recovery lines if the live demo deviates

If Gymstant encounters an error:

> Gymstant explains the problem in plain language. A nontechnical staff member can select Fix, which asks Hermes to repair the issue and retry the original assignment.

If the class software is not visible:

> Open the class software at `http://education.localhost:8000`, sign in, and continue the missed-class workflow from the last verified step.

If time is running short:

Skip the interruption test and the reasoning follow-up. Preserve the Monitor, scheduling, and Memory scenes because they communicate the strongest product differentiation.
